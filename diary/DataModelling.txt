This project is a regression problem because the goal is to predict a continuous output, which is Medicaid cost per unit for a drug. I first used Linear Regression as a simple baseline because it is easy to interpret and helps establish minimum expected performance. After that, I compared stronger tree-based models such as XGBoost and finally LightGBM because the dataset contains a mix of numeric and categorical variables and millions of rows. LightGBM was a strong choice because it handles categorical features efficiently and performs well on large structured tabular datasets. I used a train-test split with 80 percent training data and 20 percent testing data so the model could be evaluated on unseen observations. I also used log transformation on cost_per_unit because the target was heavily right-skewed and the transformation improved stability during training. I monitored training and validation behavior to avoid overfitting because the goal is generalization, not memorization. Simpler models underfit because they could not capture pricing complexity, while boosted tree models performed much better. This modeling setup gave me both interpretability and strong predictive performance.
