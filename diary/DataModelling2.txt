I used R², RMSE, and MAE as the main evaluation metrics because this project is focused on regression performance. R² helps explain how much variation in cost per unit the model can capture, while RMSE and MAE show how large prediction errors are in real units. Linear Regression served as the baseline model and helped show the minimum predictive performance expected from the feature set. XGBoost improved results by capturing non-linear relationships, but LightGBM performed best overall and became the final selected model. The final LightGBM model achieved an R² of 0.8562, RMSE of 46.8734, and MAE of 6.3614, which showed strong predictive ability on unseen test data. This means the model captured most of the pricing variation while keeping average prediction error relatively low. Feature importance also showed that utilization variables, state, therapeutic class, and manufacturer-related fields were among the strongest drivers of prediction. This supports the idea that both market behavior and product characteristics influence reimbursement prices. Compared to the baseline, LightGBM clearly improved predictive quality and gave a stronger business-facing model for the dashboard.
